## **Why I make COLLAPSE MACHINE**

### Freedom

First most important feeling I want to encounter in my game - being free to do whatever you want. Looking at horizon and wishing to go beyond it. Adventures. First months of gaming in *Minecraft* or *CubeWorld* gave that feeling of fresh vast world to explore. Where is stronghold, what's inside village, what secrets I may find over new hill in this new biomes

### Home

In each adventure game you collect stuff you find on your way. We build home to store it. Safe place to hang out with friends, drop your loot at and just ignore monster while being inside. In Valheim each time I finish new house I find a better place to build it. I have possessions in this home, and I hate moving them to new place. Having stuff to carry with me is a burden, destroying my freedom. And I knew natural way to solve it - ***make home mobile***

### Always on the move

Giant sci-fi tank is perfect place to live in. No more things holding you back. Grab your stuff inside and drive away, go steal stuff not bolted to ground. Fortress on wheels driving wasteland, your home, your freedom.
Very important was to save the felling of travel. I want to give the wheel to friend and let him drive, while I stare outside the window seeing how hills pass by, or move furniture around our mobile home. Sea Of Thieves may be the only game with something similar

### Immersion

Immersive games obviously drag me deeper into its universe. So everything must feel real, just like your home. Like you was always a part of this world. Anything you think looks interactable - it should be. You should be able to touch it, move it, break it, or use it. And it should be fun to do so. I want to make the world feel alive, and you are the part of it. Anything not doing its purpose must be redesigned or removed. It must feel like immersive simulator - Deus Ex

### Exploring

Trying to find new things must be engaging. Is modern games I just run in pre-designed path. One step aside - "oh look, an easter egg". Older games might contain secrets in absolutely random places. Tiny hole behind boxes or waterfall, or whole secret area behind bushes filled with terrorists.
I want places with vents, holes and secret passages. Like I'm a cat infiltrating whatever place I desire. And each new turn into unexpected place has tiny reward. A nice view, piece of lore from surroundings, small fight or good ol' box of junk.

### I hate UI

In recent games I caught myself playing with UI, not the game. You drop on session with random players, check out the map, see objective, run to it, see enemies on map just run around them. Finish objective and evacuate. It felt like I'm a rat in a maze, released with clear purpose to fulfill until timer runs out.
My vision is to avoid UI when possible. No inventory management and terminals reading. You only look at world, listen to sounds, look for clues, searching for anything useful. Adventures must have the unknown, and you are the one to discover it. Of course, there will be tools like map or radar, but those are items, not just magic UI floating in front of you. I still had to make health and items hotbar like that :(
If not enough space in 9 slots of hotbar - find a box, drop stuff inside the box and take the box with you. But box is real, you have to open it and consider size of items you put inside.

### In-game junk, look at my trash

In game like Fallout you collect junk all around. But you never use it. You just collect it, store it, and forget about it. For me each item might be unique. I found it on adventures, at specific place. And now it decorates my room. Takes my space. But other stuff goes into furnace as fuel. But what if that shovel could be useful? Or this funny figurine, It could be secret key. I want to collect each item and bring it to home. Hoard it like a crazy old man. Show off how my house reflects my adventures.

### Multiplayer

Everything with friends is double the fun. Hopefully, having dedicated server like in Minecraft with random adventures will be possible. But now I aim for 4+ coop with friend

### Conclusion

I need a game about adventures, while driving giant home with friends, where only wild west rules apply to anyone we encounter. With freedom to *explore, collect, destroy, and fight*. And I'm making it for myself, because I want to play it. And I hope you will too.
